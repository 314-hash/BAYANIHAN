🛠️ Before You Start — 3 Quick Checks

1
Node.js must be installed

This runs the blockchain simulation engine. Download it free at nodejs.org if you haven't already.

2
Start the local blockchain node

Open your terminal, go to the Bayanihan folder, and run:

npx hardhat node

Leave that window running. It's your "local bank" for this demo.

3
Compile the smart contracts

In a second terminal window, inside the Bayanihan folder, run:

npm run compile

This prepares all the digital rulebooks for the simulation.

What is a "Soulbound Token" (SBT)?

Think of your PhilSys National ID — it belongs exclusively to you and can't be sold or given to someone else. An SBT is a digital version of that on the blockchain. It proves you're a verified Filipino citizen in a specific role (like Farmer or Freelancer) without exposing your personal data publicly. Your identity earns you access — it can never be traded or speculated on.

🔐

Why does the farmer earn more for organic produce?

Organic farming is better for the land, healthier for Filipinos, and harder to produce. The Bayanihan smart contract automatically doubles the reward (from 10 to 20 BAYANI) when a farmer certifies their produce as organic — no middleman decides this, the code enforces it automatically. Climate-friendly practices earn an additional bonus.

How does the Escrow protect both the client and the freelancer?

Imagine hiring a web developer but being afraid they won't deliver, or a developer afraid the client won't pay. The Bayanihan smart contract acts like a trusted third party that everyone agrees to in advance. The client's budget is locked inside the digital contract. The freelancer only gets paid after submitting work AND the client approves each milestone. No one can cheat.

Plain-Language Web3 Glossary

🔍 What Do These Words Mean?

🆔
Soulbound Token (SBT)
Your permanent, non-tradeable digital ID card on the blockchain. Like a PhilSys card, it belongs only to you and proves your verified role — Farmer, Freelancer, OFW, etc. It can never be sold or faked.
📜
Smart Contract
A digital barangay ordinance that runs automatically without anyone controlling it. When conditions are met (e.g., "buyer confirmed delivery"), it releases funds to the farmer — no delays, no middlemen, no corruption possible.
🖼️
Crop NFT
A unique digital certificate tied to a specific real-world harvest. Think of it as a digital "kalusugan ng pananim" certificate — it proves this exact batch of rice or tuna came from a verified source.
🔒
Escrow
A secure digital lockbox. A client's budget is stored inside a smart contract when a project starts. The freelancer only receives payment after the client approves the work. Nobody can steal or disappear with the funds.
🪙
BAYANI Token
The community currency of the Bayanihan ecosystem — used to pay for services, vote in community decisions, and earn rewards for productive activities. It is a utility token, not an investment — earning it requires real work.
🏛️
Barangay DAO
A digital democracy tool for community decisions. Citizens stake BAYANI tokens to gain voting power on local infrastructure proposals (e.g., solar streetlights). A voting weight cap prevents wealthy individuals from dominating decisions.
🏢
Real-World Asset (RWA)
A physical community facility (like a drying warehouse or solar microgrid) that is digitally divided into fractional shares. Owning shares gives you utility discounts on that facility — not cash dividends — to stay within SEC rules.
🛡️
Post-Quantum Keys
Ultra-secure encryption that protects your digital identity even from future quantum computers (which could break today's encryption). Bayanihan lets you rotate your keys to stay protected as technology advances.
⭐
AI Reputation Score
A score out of 100 that measures your track record on the platform — on-time delivery, ratings from clients, dispute resolution results. A higher score automatically reduces your platform fees — it rewards trusted contributors.
